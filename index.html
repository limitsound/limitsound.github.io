Installing — read this first

LimitSound is an independent app distributed outside the App Store, so the first launch needs one extra step. This is normal for indie Mac apps.

Open the downloaded LimitSound.dmg and drag LimitSound into Applications.

Double-click LimitSound. macOS will say it "could not verify the app is free of malware" — close that dialog.

macOS shows this for every app that isn't registered with Apple's paid developer program.

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click "Open Anyway". Confirm with your password.

You only do this once — updates install automatically afterwards.

Look for the headphones icon in your menu bar. Connect your AirPods and you're protected.

Prefer the Terminal? This one-liner skips step 2–3:

Honest fine print

macOS doesn't expose the true sound-pressure level of headphones to third-party apps — only Apple can do that. LimitSound's dB scale is a calibrated approximation anchored at ≈100 dB for full volume: the 5 dB steps are accurate relative to each other, while the absolute level depends on your headphone model, the track, and fit. It's a strong safety net, not a lab instrument.
